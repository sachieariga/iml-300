concept
final: take care

In this project called "Take Care," I decided to create a tribute for my mother,
who was the main person who raised me throughout childhood. Through the subtopics
of "people," "places," "things," and "words," I wish to describe the special
relationship that we had. A good chunk of the project creation included
poem-making, which was a new medium for me. I experimented with different CSS
effects on different pages, and added some of my iPad drawings as well. I went
with a winter theme for the cover slide because it ties into the
final "words" page.

#3: networked justice

concept
final: sushi culture

This project is an informational website that is centered around traditional
Japanese sushi. I display the dish as a craft of precision and a representation
of Japanese creativity. By moving abroad for the first time last year, I have
been able to consume a lot of fusion cuisine; however, I wished to create an
artistic platform that appreciates an aspect of traditional Japanese cuisine
specifically. Although I believe that authentic Japanese sushi will be appreciated
worldwide for years to come, I wanted to educate others about the skill that is
required to make sushi, the etiquette that is encouraged while eating it, and the
history behind the dish itself. The subtopics include “history,” “types of sushi,”
“making sushi,” and “eating sushi." Despite the site’s informational nature, I wanted
to make the layout relatively simple without it being too wordy. I also gave myself
a challenge by drawing all the visual elements on the website.

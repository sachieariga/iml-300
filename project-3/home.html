in 2021, i moved outside of my country for the first time.
growing up in tokyo, i had the privilege of fitting in in terms of my looks.
for the first time in my life, i wasn't part of the racial majority.
i found myself telling people about my culture and where i came from.

i still find myself gravitating towards people, food, and places that remind me of home.
it's also nice when other people appreciate my culture.

i find that a lot of people appreciate sushi,
but i feel as though not many people know about japanese food ettiquette and food history.
this is why i wanted to create a website on sushi,
but not the lobster roll, dragon roll, or california roll.
also not about the sushi burrito, or sushi taco.
i love eating those too, but i wanted to dedicate this website for traditional sushi.
in doing so, i hope people can learn more not just about sushi
but also about japanese etiquette and traditional culture.
enjoy!
